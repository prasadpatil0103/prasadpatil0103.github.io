SmartShop Analytics Platform: Real-Time E-Commerce Pipeline

Real-Time Streaming & Analytics Engineering · 2026

Problem

E-commerce operations need real-time visibility into order flow, revenue, and fulfillment. Batch pipelines create latency that delays business decisions. This project builds a fully streaming architecture from ingestion to dashboard.

Architecture

  • Kafka Producer: Simulates real-time order streaming from the Olist Brazilian e-commerce dataset (99K+ orders).
  • PySpark Streaming Consumer: Processes and enriches order events in real time, writing to Delta Lake with ACID transactions.
  • dbt Transformation Layer: 4 modular models — daily revenue, top products, order status summary, and customer analytics by region.
  • Databricks Dashboard: Live BI dashboard exposing $15.42M revenue, 97% delivery rate, and regional/category breakdowns.

Key Results

  • $15.42M total revenue tracked across 96K+ orders.
  • 97.02% successful order delivery rate.
  • Health & Beauty identified as top revenue category at $1.23M.
  • São Paulo dominates with $5.77M — 37% of total revenue.

Engineering Decisions

  • Why Kafka? Decouples the producer and consumer, enabling independent scaling and fault tolerance in the streaming layer.
  • Why Delta Lake? ACID transactions and time travel give the data lake the reliability guarantees of a database.
  • Why dbt on Databricks? Version-controlled, testable transformations on a cloud-native compute platform.
  • Why Docker? Reproducible local Kafka + Zookeeper environment eliminates setup friction.

Financial Fraud Detection Analytics Platform

Fraud Analytics & Machine Learning · 2026

Problem

Financial institutions face a 3.5% fraud rate across hundreds of thousands of transactions. Manual review is unsustainable. This platform builds a complete analytics and ML system to detect, explain, and quantify fraud exposure.

Approach

  • Star Schema ETL: Ingested 590,540 IEEE-CIS transactions into a SQLite star schema with fact_transactions and 4 dimension tables (dim_card, dim_date, dim_email, dim_device).
  • SQL Analytics Layer: Built 3 production views using CTEs and window functions — fraud summary, high-risk segments ranked by card network and hour, and transaction velocity analysis.
  • XGBoost Classifier: Applied SMOTE to correct 27.6:1 class imbalance. Final model achieved AUC 0.81, 76% recall, 72% precision — baseline logistic regression was 0.59 AUC.
  • SHAP Explainability: Identified TransactionAmt, card velocity, and transaction hour as top fraud signals, providing interpretable outputs for business stakeholders.

Key Findings

  • Discover cards carry a 7.7% fraud rate — more than double Visa (3.5%).
  • Hours 5–9 UTC represent a high-risk window peaking at 10.5% fraud rate at hour 7.
  • Discover credit cards at hour 11 reach a 27.1% fraud rate — the highest single segment.
  • $3.08M in fraudulent transactions identified across $79.7M total volume.
  • Chi-square test confirms card network is a statistically significant fraud predictor (χ²=368.90, p≈0).

RAG Job Copilot: Cloud-Native Job Intelligence System

IST 615 · AWS Cloud & NLP · Spring 2026

Problem

Job seekers struggle to identify which skills are truly in demand without manually reading dozens of postings. This system implements a RAG pipeline over real job descriptions to surface structured skill insights through natural language queries.

Architecture

  • Storage: 30 real job descriptions (.txt) stored in Amazon S3, covering Data Engineer, Data Scientist, and Data Analyst roles (10 each).
  • Processing: AWS Lambda (Python 3.11) loads job files, vectorizes with TF-IDF, and ranks by cosine similarity to the user query.
  • API Layer: AWS API Gateway exposes a REST endpoint — POST /prod/query returns top skills and matching job metadata as structured JSON.
  • Frontend: Streamlit UI with an analytics dashboard showing cross-role skill overlap, seniority distribution, and top skills by role using Plotly.
  • Skill Normalization: Aliases unified to canonical names (e.g. pyspark/apache spark → spark, k8s → kubernetes).

Results

  • 52 unique skills extracted across 30 job descriptions — 6.6 avg skills per job.
  • Role and seniority auto-detected from filename and job description text.
  • Serverless deployment on AWS Lambda with 512MB memory, 30s timeout.

Supply Chain Intelligence Platform

Supply Chain Analytics & Demand Forecasting · 2026

Problem

A global retail company has a 57.3% late delivery rate across 180K orders but cannot identify root causes or forecast future demand. This platform answers both questions end to end.

Key Findings

  • First Class shipping: 100% late delivery rate across 27,814 shipments — a systematic scheduling failure, not random variation.
  • Top 3 products hold the #1 revenue-at-risk position in all 23 global regions simultaneously — fixing their fulfilment recovers top risk across all geographies.
  • Zero improvement in late delivery rate over 37 months — the 3-year flat line is the finding.
  • Top 25% of customers by spend (generating 58% of revenue) receive the same late rate as bottom-tier customers — zero service differentiation.
  • Root cause validated statistically: scheduling gap (correlation -0.40 with days_late), not order characteristics.

Technical Approach

  • Star schema in SQLite with 4 dimension tables across 180,519 fact rows.
  • Advanced SQL with LAG(), RANK() PARTITION BY, NTILE(4), and running totals.
  • Chi-square (χ²=41,856, p≈0) and Kruskal-Wallis tests for statistical validation.
  • Prophet demand forecasting: MAPE 0.6%, MAE 28.6 orders/month — well within 95% confidence intervals.

Forecasting Fantasy Football with Machine Learning

IST 718 — Big Data Analytics · Syracuse University · 2026

Problem

Fantasy football managers rely on projected points to make lineup decisions. This project investigates whether those projections can be improved using historical player performance data, player age, and positional stats across 8 NFL seasons.

Technical Approach

  • Data Infrastructure: Pulled 79K player-week observations (2018–2025) from the Sleeper API, stored in a PySpark database as a season-partitioned Parquet table.
  • Year-to-Year Consistency: Computed lagged correlations per position — WRs most stable (0.69), QBs most volatile (0.63) due to rushing variance.
  • Global GBT Model: Single gradient boosting model across all positions — RMSE 62.6, MAE 36.8, R² 48.9%. Prior season PPR was the strongest single predictor by far.
  • Position-Specific Models: Separate GBT models per position. TEs most predictable (RMSE 39.98), QBs highest variance (RMSE 89.36).

2026 Projection Highlights

  • QB: Jaxson Dart projected at 362.5 PPR points — model identifies age trajectory over prior-season output.

  • WR: Amon-Ra St. Brown tops at 263.3 projected points.
  • TE: Trey McBride leads at 241.2, Travis Kelce at 228.5 despite age.

NYC Taxi: Scalable Data Engineering Pipeline

Syracuse University | IST 782 | Data Engineering Track | Feb 2026 – Mar 2026

Problem

Raw NYC taxi data arrived as high-volume records with inconsistent formats and invalid entries, making it unusable for analytics or machine learning without a scalable transformation layer.

Approach — Medallion Architecture

  • Bronze Layer: Ingested raw NYC taxi data into Snowflake without transformation, preserving source fidelity for reprocessing and auditability.
  • Silver Layer: Used PySpark distributed processing across 5.7M+ records to apply schema standardization, quality validation, and cleansing rules.
  • Gold Layer: Built modular dbt models to produce curated datasets for demand, fare, pickup, and distance analysis.
  • Serving Layer: Exposed trusted Gold-layer datasets through FastAPI for dashboard and application consumption.

Results

  • Processed and transformed 5.7M+ records through a scalable ELT workflow.
  • Reduced invalid records before downstream analytics through Silver-layer quality enforcement.
  • Produced analytics-ready and ML-ready Gold datasets for demand, fare, and trip behavior analysis.
  • Enabled real-time access via FastAPI endpoints.

From Mumbai to Syracuse: Two Years of Building, Breaking, and Rethinking Data

What I Expected Coming In

I joined the MS in Applied Data Science program with two years at Tata Consultancy Services behind me. I knew how to write SQL, build pipelines, and ship working systems. What I lacked was the why — the architectural reasoning, the statistical foundations, and the design principles that separate a brittle pipeline from a resilient one.

Three Projects That Shaped My Thinking

NYC Taxi ELT Pipeline (IST 782). Building a full Medallion architecture — Bronze ingestion, PySpark Silver validation across 5.7M records, dbt Gold modeling, and FastAPI serving — forced me to justify every architectural decision in sequence.

Healthcare Hub (Fall 2025). Designing a Kimball star schema in Snowflake with automated dbt quality testing changed my view of data governance. Quality enforcement before data reaches a reporting layer is what makes the reporting layer trustworthy.

Crime Prediction in Syracuse (IST 707). Building a model that predicts geographic crime risk required thinking about bias, fairness, and harm in ways I had never formalized before.
