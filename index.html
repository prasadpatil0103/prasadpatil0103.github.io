Goal

Building a robust ELT pipeline using dbt and Snowflake to transform raw business data into actionable Power BI intelligence for Sales, Inventory, and Procurement.

The Challenge

Manual SQL transformations often lead to "spaghetti code" and inconsistent metrics. The goal was to implement an Analytics Engineering workflow that ensures data quality and provides a "Single Source of Truth" across three disparate business processes.

The Technical Architecture (The Modern Data Stack)

  • Storage & Staging (Snowflake): Centralized raw transactional data in Snowflake, leveraging its elastic scaling to handle high-volume Sales and Procurement records.

Energy Hub: Predictive Consumption & Grid Reliability

Predictive Analytics & Climate Modeling

Goal

Building an end-to-end predictive analytics platform using dbt, Snowflake, and R to forecast energy demand spikes and prevent grid failure under extreme global warming scenarios.

The Challenge

South Carolina's energy grid faces significant risk during peak July heatwaves. As global temperatures rise, demand for electricity to cool homes increases exponentially, potentially leading to catastrophic blackouts. The "Energy Hub" was designed to quantify the "Temperature Sensitivity" of residential loads and build a model capable of predicting consumption when temperatures exceed historical norms.

The Technical Architecture (Modern Data Stack)

I implemented a full-stack ELT and Predictive pipeline:

  • Data Ingestion (Snowflake): Integrated high-velocity housing characteristics, weather conditions, and energy usage datasets. Data was fetched using the arrow library and optimized via parquet functions for high-speed processing.
  • Analytics Engineering (dbt):
    • Staging Layer: Cleaned and standardized raw data by County and Building ID, ensuring consistency across disparate weather and housing sources.
    • Transformation Layer: Developed modular models to correlate variables and identify consumption patterns, serving as the "Feature Store" for the machine learning pipeline.
  • Predictive Modeling (R): Exported cleaned marts to an R-based environment for advanced statistical modeling and "what-if" simulations.

Advanced Modeling & Predictive Analytics

This project focused on moving beyond descriptive statistics to create a robust forecasting engine:

  • Support Vector Machine (SVM) Regression: Implemented an SVM model with a radial basis function (RBF) kernel to capture the non-linear relationship between ambient dry-bulb temperatures and cooling system intensity.
    • Model Accuracy: Achieved a high test-set accuracy of 93.16%.
    • Error Metrics: Maintained a Root Mean Square Error (RMSE) of 0.708, indicating exceptional model stability and reliability for utility-scale planning.
  • Global Warming Simulation (5Â° Raise): A core feature was simulating a 5Â° temperature increase. The model predicted a 32% exponential surge in energy demand for every 5Â° rise, allowing the grid to prepare for "worst-case" climate scenarios.
  • Total Forecasted Load: The model calculated a total peak consumption of 894.39 KWH for the targeted test region under elevated heat conditions.

Key Insights & Analysis

Model validation showing strong alignment between actual energy usage and SVM predictions, with minimal outliers and high accuracy (93.16%).

Efficiency benchmarking revealing pre-1990 residential units consume disproportionately more energy per square foot, supporting targeted modernization programs.

Climate scenario simulation showing energy consumption spikes (blue line) with volatility patterns under a 5Â° temperature increase, with red trend line indicating baseline consumption for comparison.

The Analytics Suite & Insights

  • Temperature & Consumption Trends: Visualized the direct correlation between dry-bulb temperature fluctuations and energy spikes, specifically pinpointing July as the highest risk month for grid strain.
  • Efficiency Benchmarking (Old vs. New Housing): Analyzed energy consumption per square foot based on building age. Identified that pre-1990 residential units contribute disproportionately to peak loads, providing a data-backed case for targeted appliance modernization programs.
  • R Shiny Interactive Dashboard: Developed a functional web application allowing stakeholders to manipulate temperature sliders and see real-time predicted energy shifts, facilitating rapid decision-making for load-shedding protocols.

Key Technical Outcomes

  • Grid Reliability Intelligence: Identified that increasing fan usage alongside cooling systems allows for higher thermostat settings without losing comfort, potentially flattening the "peak demand" curve and saving the grid from failure.
  • Data-Driven Recommendations:
    • Lighting Transition: Proven that replacing dry-bulbs with LED bulbs can significantly offset the 32% energy surge predicted by the model.
